## 📊 **The Complete Safety Rating System**

We rate every baby food product on a **A+ to F scale** based on heavy metal levels:

| Grade | Arsenic | Lead | Cadmium | Mercury | Verdict |
|-------|---------|------|---------|---------|---------|
| A+ | < 1 ppb | < 0.5 ppb | < 1 ppb | < 0.2 ppb | **Exceptional** |
| A | < 5 ppb | < 1 ppb | < 2 ppb | < 0.5 ppb | **Very Safe** |
| B | 5-10 ppb | 1-2 ppb | 2-5 ppb | 0.5-1 ppb | **Acceptable** |
| C | 10-25 ppb | 2-5 ppb | 5-10 ppb | 1-2 ppb | **Caution** |
| D | 25-50 ppb | 5-10 ppb | 10-25 ppb | 2-5 ppb | **Risky** |
| F | > 50 ppb | > 10 ppb | > 25 ppb | > 5 ppb | **Avoid** |

**Our rule:** Only A+ to B grade products are recommended. C and below should be avoided, especially for infants under 12 months.

## ⚠️ **Red Flags to Watch For**

Even within "safe" brands, watch out for:

| Red Flag | Why It's Bad | What to Do |
|----------|--------------|------------|
| Rice, brown rice, rice flour in first 3 ingredients | High arsenic | Skip it |
| "Whole grain" claims without specifics | Often brown rice | Check ingredients |

| No barcode on package | Can't verify with scanner | Contact manufacturer for testing data (they rarely provide) |
| "Detox" or "cleansing" claims | Marketing hype, not safety | Ignore |

## 📦 **What About Homemade Baby Food?**

Making your own purees from organic produce is **the gold standard** for safety—IF you control the ingredient sourcing.

**Pros:**
- You choose ingredients with low heavy metal potential (avoid rice, select produce wisely)
- No processing contamination
- Fresher nutrients

**Cons:**
- Time-consuming
- Risk of spoilage/bacterial growth if not handled properly
- Limited shelf life

**Our recommendation:** If you have time, homemade is great. Just **avoid rice-based cereals** even when homemade. Use organic oats, quinoa, millet instead.
